## Why It Matters

Amazon buyers are trained to skip promotional language. They've seen ten thousand listings that call their product "premium," "innovative," and "game-changing." The instant they detect this language — consciously or not — their trust drops and their finger moves to the back button.

AI-generated listing copy fails for the same reason it fails everywhere: it optimizes for *sounding like a good listing* rather than *actually communicating product truth*. It produces the statistical average of "professional product copy," which is word salad made of hype.

Good Amazon copy works for two distinct audiences simultaneously:
- **The algorithm:** needs keyword-dense, structured text in the right fields
- **The buyer:** needs specific, believable, benefit-forward language that answers "why this one?"

The humanizer makes copy work for both. Not by compromising one for the other, but by understanding that specific, factual, direct language satisfies both audiences better than inflated promotional text.

## The 24 Patterns — Amazon Edition

### CONTENT PATTERNS

---

### Pattern 1: Significance Inflation
**What it is:** Adding statements about how the product "represents," "marks," or "reflects" something bigger. Puffing up importance with framing language.

**AI vocabulary to kill:**
`stands as` / `serves as` / `is a testament to` / `pivotal` / `vital` / `crucial` / `underscores` / `reflects broader` / `evolving landscape` / `marking a shift` / `deeply rooted` / `enduring` / `setting the stage for` / `indelible mark` / `focal point`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Every product cannot be "a testament to our commitment to quality." It says nothing. It fills 8 characters of your 200-character title limit with noise.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "This ergonomic chair represents a pivotal advancement in workplace wellness, underscoring the importance of proper posture." | "Lumbar support adjusts in 3 directions. Seat depth slides 2 inches forward for shorter torsos." |
| "A testament to our enduring commitment to quality craftsmanship." | "Triple-stitched seams. Full-grain leather. 5-year warranty." |
| "This knife marks a turning point in home cooking." | "German 1.4116 steel. Rockwell hardness 56-58. Full tang." |

**Rule:** State the spec or benefit directly. Never frame why it matters — let the fact speak.

---

### Pattern 2: Notability / Authority Name-Dropping
**What it is:** Claiming the product has been recognized, featured, or praised by vague authorities or media without specifics.

**AI vocabulary to kill:**
`as seen in` / `featured in leading publications` / `recognized by experts` / `endorsed by professionals` / `widely regarded` / `award-winning` (without naming the award)

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Amazon explicitly prohibits claims of "best seller," "#1," "award-winning," and comparative superlatives without substantiation. Vague authority claims may trigger listing suppression.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Recognized by health professionals worldwide for its superior formulation." | "NSF Certified for Sport. Batch-tested for 270+ banned substances. Certificate on file." |
| "As seen in top fitness publications. Trusted by athletes everywhere." | "Used by the US Olympic weightlifting team (see brand page for details)." |
| "Award-winning design praised by industry experts." | "Red Dot Design Award 2023 — citation available at brand.com/awards" |

**Rule:** Name the specific award, publication, or certifying body. If you can't, cut it.

---

### Pattern 3: Superficial -ing Clauses (Fake Depth)
**What it is:** Tacking present-participle phrases onto sentences to make them seem more analytical or meaningful. The -ing clause usually adds nothing factual.

**AI vocabulary to kill:**
`highlighting` / `showcasing` / `reflecting` / `symbolizing` / `contributing to` / `fostering` / `ensuring` / `cultivating` / `emphasizing` / `encompassing` / `underscoring`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Bullet character limits are precious. An -ing clause that adds no fact wastes 20–40 characters that could carry a real spec.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Featuring advanced moisture-wicking technology, ensuring comfort throughout your workout, reflecting our commitment to performance." | "Moisture-wicking fabric pulls sweat away from skin. Dries in under 20 minutes." |
| "Our vacuum insulation keeps beverages cold for 24 hours, showcasing the quality of our double-wall construction." | "Double-wall vacuum insulation. Cold 24 hours, hot 12 hours." |
| "Premium stainless steel construction, contributing to long-term durability and reliability." | "304 stainless steel. Rust-resistant. Dishwasher safe." |

**Rule:** If the -ing clause doesn't add a fact, number, or specific claim — delete it.

---

### Pattern 4: Promotional / Ad-like Language
**What it is:** Adjectives that sound like an advertisement. Words that tell the customer something is good rather than showing them why.

**AI vocabulary to kill:**
`premium` / `luxury` / `stunning` / `breathtaking` / `vibrant` / `groundbreaking` / `revolutionary` / `innovative` / `boasts` / `nestled` / `commitment to excellence` / `must-have` / `perfect` / `amazing` / `incredible` / `rich` (figurative) / `superior` / `exceptional`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** These words trigger buyer skepticism immediately. Every competitor uses "premium quality." The buyer reads it as noise. Plus, many superlatives violate Amazon's listing guidelines.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Experience the stunning luxury of our premium handcrafted leather wallet, boasting groundbreaking RFID technology." | "Full-grain leather. 6 card slots, 2 cash compartments. Blocks RFID signals at 13.56 MHz." |
| "Revolutionary design offers incredible comfort for all-day wear." | "Memory foam insole conforms to foot arch. Rebounding foam returns to shape after 50,000+ steps." |
| "Our premium supplements feature a superior blend of quality ingredients." | "2,000mg Vitamin C per serving. No fillers. Third-party tested by Labdoor — grade A." |

**Rule:** Replace every vague positive adjective with the specific fact that earns it.

---

### Pattern 5: Vague Attributions / Weasel Words
**What it is:** Attributing claims to unnamed, unverifiable sources to add credibility without providing any.

**AI vocabulary to kill:**
`experts say` / `studies show` / `customers love` / `professionals recommend` / `many users report` / `industry leaders agree` / `research suggests` (without citation)

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Amazon can suppress listings for unsubstantiated health claims or unsupported comparative claims. Vague attribution is both ineffective as copy and a compliance risk.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Experts recommend this supplement for daily immune support." | "Contains 1,000mg Vitamin C — the dose used in the 2020 NIH immune function meta-analysis." |
| "Studies show our mattress improves sleep quality." | "CertiPUR-US certified foam. 120-night sleep trial. Over 12,000 reviews averaging 4.6 stars." |
| "Many customers report noticeable results within 2 weeks." | "Based on our 500-person beta program, 78% of participants reported reduced soreness within 14 days." |

**Rule:** Name the source, the study, or the specific data point. If you don't have one, cut the claim.

---

### Pattern 6: Formulaic "Challenges" / "Future Prospects" / "Conclusion" Sections
**What it is:** Boilerplate structural sections that exist because AI writing defaults to essay structure.

**AI patterns to kill:**
`Despite challenges...` / `continues to thrive` / `looking ahead` / `future outlook` / `in conclusion` / `to summarize` / `ultimately`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** These never appear in good listings. If you see them in a draft, it means the copy was assembled rather than written.

Substitute: Every character spent on structural boilerplate should carry a spec, use case, or trust signal.

### LANGUAGE AND GRAMMAR PATTERNS

---

### Pattern 7: AI Vocabulary Cluster
**What it is:** High-frequency words that appear disproportionately in post-2023 AI-generated text. When two or more co-occur in a single bullet, the text reads as machine-generated.

**The word list:**
`additionally` / `align with` / `crucial` / `delve` / `emphasizing` / `enduring` / `enhance` / `fostering` / `garner` / `highlight` (verb) / `interplay` / `intricate` / `key` (adjective) / `landscape` (abstract) / `pivotal` / `showcase` / `tapestry` / `testament` / `underscore` / `valuable` / `vibrant`

**Detection rule:** Scan the full listing draft. Mark every instance. If any bullet contains 2+, rewrite from scratch — it was generated, not written.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Additionally, the intricate design showcases our commitment to craftsmanship, enhancing your culinary experience." | "The handle is full tang — extends through the blade for balance. Bolster is triple-riveted." |
| "Key features include vibrant color options that enhance the overall aesthetic." | "Available in 8 colors: Midnight Black, Warm White, Slate Grey, Navy, Forest Green, Rust, Cream, Blush." |

---

### Pattern 8: Copula Avoidance (Dodging "is / are / has")
**What it is:** Substituting elaborate verb phrases for the simple copula verb.

**Words that replace "is/has" unnecessarily:**
`serves as` / `stands as` / `marks` (when meaning "is") / `represents` / `boasts` / `features` / `offers` (when meaning "has")

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Makes copy feel like marketing spin. "This knife is built for precision" beats "This knife serves as the ideal precision instrument" — both in authenticity and character count.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "This blender serves as the perfect tool for smoothie lovers." | "This blender handles frozen fruit, ice, and whole nuts without stopping." |
| "The bag features a reinforced base and boasts weatherproof zippers." | "Reinforced base. YKK weatherproof zippers. Holds 40L." |
| "Our supplement stands as a comprehensive solution for joint health." | "Glucosamine 1,500mg + Chondroitin 1,200mg + MSM 1,000mg per serving." |

---

### Pattern 9: Negative Parallelisms
**What it is:** "It's not just X, it's Y" constructions. Used to build up a claim by contrasting it against a lesser version of itself.

**Patterns to kill:**
`It's not just...` / `Not merely...` / `More than just...` / `Beyond simply...` / `This isn't just a...`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Wastes characters. Buries the actual claim. Sounds like a TV infomercial.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "It's not just a water bottle — it's a lifestyle tool for serious hydration." | "32oz. Keeps drinks cold 24 hours, hot 12 hours. Fits standard cup holders." |
| "Not merely a coffee maker — a complete morning ritual system." | "Brews 1–12 cups. Programmable 24 hours in advance. Thermal carafe keeps coffee hot 4 hours." |

---

### Pattern 10: Rule of Three Overuse
**What it is:** Forcing all descriptions, benefits, and features into groups of three because it "sounds complete."

**Pattern:** `durable, reliable, and versatile` / `innovation, quality, and value` / `for work, play, and adventure`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Lists of three vague adjectives carry no information. Replace with actual numbers and facts.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Perfect for camping, hiking, and outdoor adventures. Durable, lightweight, and reliable." | "Rated for 3-season use down to 20°F / -7°C. Weighs 1.2 lbs. Stuff sack included." |
| "Designed for innovation, performance, and style." | Cut entirely. State what it actually does. |

**Rule:** Use the natural number of real points. Two facts beat three vague adjectives.

---

### Pattern 11: Synonym Cycling (Elegant Variation)
**What it is:** Avoiding word repetition by cycling through synonyms for the same thing. Produces confusing copy because the reader isn't sure if the synonyms mean the same thing.

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** "Product" / "item" / "unit" / "piece" / "purchase" cycling is a dead giveaway. Pick the right noun and use it.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "This product arrives fully assembled. The unit features solid oak construction. Each piece is hand-sanded." | "Arrives fully assembled. Solid oak construction. Hand-sanded finish." |
| "Our item ships in 2 days. The product includes all hardware. Your purchase comes with a warranty." | "Ships in 2 days. Includes all mounting hardware and 2-year warranty." |

---

### Pattern 12: False Ranges
**What it is:** "From X to Y" constructions where X and Y aren't on a meaningful spectrum, used to create an impression of versatility.

**Pattern:** `suitable for everything from...to...` / `from casual to professional` / `from beginner to expert`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** Tells the buyer nothing specific. What does "casual to professional" mean? Who is the actual buyer?

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Suitable for everything from casual weekend use to professional applications." | "Machine washable. Used by Amazon warehouse staff (bulk orders available)." |
| "Perfect for everyone from beginner cooks to professional chefs." | "8-inch blade. Balanced for medium grip strength. Used in culinary school programs." |

### STYLE PATTERNS

---

### Pattern 13: Em Dash Overuse
**What it is:** Using em dashes (—) for dramatic pauses or to create a punchy effect, beyond what's natural.

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** More than one em dash in a bullet looks like infomercial copy.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "The knife—crafted from German steel—features a full tang—ensuring lifetime durability." | "German 1.4116 steel blade. Full tang extends through handle. Lifetime warranty." |
| "Our bag—designed for urban professionals—offers 40L capacity—perfect for a 3-day trip." | "40L capacity. Fits overhead bin. Suits 3-day travel." |

**Rule:** Zero to one em dash per bullet maximum. Prefer periods.

---

### Pattern 14: Boldface Overuse
**What it is:** Bolding random phrases throughout bullets as if every phrase deserves emphasis.

**Amazon-specific rule:** Bold in bullets is not standard practice. In A+ Content and descriptions, use bold only for headings or true key terms, not decoratively.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "**Premium quality** materials with **advanced technology** for **superior performance**." | "1050D ballistic nylon shell. YKK #10 zippers rated for 50,000 open/close cycles." |

---

### Pattern 15: Inline-Header Bullet Format
**What it is:** Bullets that start with a bolded header followed by a colon, then a generic sentence. This is the most common AI listing structure.

**Pattern:**
- **Comfort:** Ergonomically designed for all-day wear
- **Durability:** Built with reinforced materials  
- **Style:** Available in multiple colors

**Why it fails:** The headers add nothing. "Comfort:" followed by a vague sentence is worse than just writing the actual spec.

**Rewrite approach:** Lead with the specific feature, follow with the specific benefit or number.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| **Comfort:** Ergonomically designed for all-day wear | Contoured footbed matches natural arch. Breathable mesh liner. Size runs true — half-size up for wide feet. |
| **Durability:** Built with reinforced stitching | Reinforced stitching at toe box and heel. Sole tested to 500,000 flex cycles. |
| **Style:** Available in 8 modern colors | 8 colors: Black, White, Grey, Navy, Olive, Brown, Rust, Cream. |

**Note on Amazon policy (2024 update):** Amazon now explicitly prohibits bullets beginning with ALL CAPS headers. The **Bold:** header format is not explicitly banned but creates the same readability issue.

---

### Pattern 16: Title Case in Headings
**What it is:** Capitalizing All Main Words In Every Heading.

**Amazon-specific rule:** Amazon's own style guide uses sentence case for section headings in A+ Content. For listing copy, this is less critical — but Title Case bullets sound corporate and stiff.

---

### Pattern 17: Emojis in Bullets
**What it is:** Using ✅ 🚀 💡 ⭐ to decorate bullets and look "eye-catching."

**Amazon-specific rule:** Amazon's product title guidelines prohibit emojis. While bullets don't have an explicit ban, emojis signal low-quality listings to buyers. Amazon has been known to auto-suppress listings with emojis in titles.

**Rule:** No emojis anywhere in listing copy. Not in titles, bullets, descriptions, or A+ Content.

---

### Pattern 18: Curly Quotation Marks
**What it is:** Using "smart" curly quotes ("like this") from Word/Google Docs instead of straight quotes ("like this").

**Amazon-specific rule:** Flat file uploads can corrupt curly quotes into characters that display incorrectly on the listing. Always use straight quotes in flat file copy.

---

### COMMUNICATION PATTERNS

---

### Pattern 19: Chatbot Artifacts
**What it is:** Conversational phrases that belong in a chat interface, not in product copy.

**Words to kill everywhere in listing drafts:**
`I hope this helps` / `Feel free to` / `Of course!` / `Certainly!` / `Would you like to` / `Let me know if` / `Here is your listing` / `As an AI` / `I've created`

**Rule:** These must be stripped before any listing content is presented. They indicate the copy came from a generation pass and wasn't reviewed.

---

### Pattern 20: Knowledge-Cutoff Disclaimers
**What it is:** Hedging claims because the model isn't sure they're current.

**AI patterns to kill:**
`as of [date]` / `based on available information` / `while specific details are limited` / `to the best of my knowledge`

**Amazon-specific application:** If certifications or claims can't be verified, either confirm them or cut them. "This product may potentially be CE certified" is worse than saying nothing and invites suppression for unsubstantiated claims.

---

### Pattern 21: Sycophantic Tone
**What it is:** Excessive warmth toward the customer that reads as trying too hard.

**AI patterns to kill:**
`We're passionate about your experience` / `We care deeply about` / `You'll love` / `Great choice` / `Designed with you in mind` / `Because you deserve`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** These phrases waste character count. They also signal to experienced Amazon buyers that the listing has no real substance to offer.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "We're passionate about quality and care deeply about your experience." | "30-day return policy, no questions asked. US-based support, 7 days a week." |
| "Because you deserve the best, we made this with only premium ingredients." | State the ingredients. |

---

### FILLER AND HEDGING

---

### Pattern 22: Filler Phrases
**What it is:** Verbose constructions that can be shortened without losing meaning.

**Amazon-specific replacements:**

| Kill | Replace with |
|------|-------------|
| "In order to achieve the best results" | "For best results" |
| "Due to the fact that it is waterproof" | "It's waterproof" / "Waterproof to IPX7" |
| "Has the ability to adjust the height" | "Height adjusts" |
| "It is important to note that" | Cut entirely |
| "In the event of a problem" | "If something goes wrong" |
| "The product is capable of holding" | "Holds" |
| "Provides the user with" | "Gives you" |
| "Is designed to be used for" | "Works for" |
| "In order to maximize performance" | "For best performance" |
| "Made from high-quality materials including" | "Made from [specific material]" |

---

### Pattern 23: Excessive Hedging (with Amazon Exceptions)
**What it is:** Over-qualifying statements to the point of saying nothing.

**General pattern to kill:**
`could potentially` / `may possibly` / `might be considered` / `in some cases` (stacked qualifiers)

**CRITICAL AMAZON EXCEPTION — Do NOT over-humanize health and supplement claims:**

Amazon and the FDA require specific qualified language in regulated categories. The following is legally *required* and must not be stripped:

- Supplements: **"These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."** — mandatory disclaimer
- Health claims: `"may help support"` / `"supports healthy..."` — required FTC/FDA phrasing. Removing "may" and writing "supports" as a certainty can trigger enforcement.
- Pesticide/chemical products: regulatory language must be preserved verbatim

**Rule:** Strip excessive hedging from *feature claims* (specs, dimensions, materials). Preserve required regulatory hedging in health, supplement, and safety claims.

---

### Pattern 24: Generic Positive Conclusions
**What it is:** Vague upbeat endings that say nothing specific.

**AI patterns to kill:**
`The perfect addition to your home` / `Elevate your lifestyle` / `Transform your daily routine` / `A must-have for every household` / `The last [product] you'll ever need`

**Amazon-specific failure mode:** These phrases eat character count in bullets and descriptions without providing any information. Amazon buyers don't need encouragement — they need information to make a decision.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "The perfect addition to any kitchen, this knife will transform your cooking experience." | "Includes 10-year edge guarantee. Send back for free resharpening." |
| "A must-have for every home gym. Elevate your fitness journey today." | "Fits under standard beds (10-inch clearance minimum). Weight capacity: 300 lbs per rack." |

## Category-Specific Tone Calibration

The humanizer rules apply universally, but *voice* calibrates to category. These are not exceptions to the 24 patterns — they're how you apply specificity in each context.

### Electronics / Tech
- Specs are the content. Lead with numbers: voltage, wattage, compatibility matrix, standards (USB-C 3.2 Gen 2, Wi-Fi 6E, Bluetooth 5.3)
- No "cutting-edge" or "next-generation" — say what generation and what it enables
- Compatibility is a trust signal: "Compatible with iPhone 14/15 and Samsung Galaxy S22/S23/S24"

### Health, Supplements, Beauty
- Ingredient amounts, not impressions: "500mg Ashwagandha (KSM-66 extract, 5% withanolides)"
- Third-party certifications are the strongest trust signal: NSF, USP, Labdoor, Informed Sport
- Required disclaimers must stay verbatim (see Pattern 23)
- No disease claims whatsoever — only "supports" / "helps maintain"

### Apparel / Footwear
- Sizing truth is the #1 conversion factor: "Runs true to size. Half-size up for wide feet."
- Material composition: "78% cotton, 18% polyester, 4% spandex"
- Care instructions in bullets reduce returns: "Machine wash cold, tumble dry low, no bleach"

### Home & Kitchen
- Dimensions matter: always include L × W × H and weight
- Compatibility specs: "Fits standard US outlets (110V). Induction compatible."
- Capacity specifics: "Holds up to 6 quarts / 5.7 liters"

### Automotive
- Year/Make/Model/Trim compatibility is mandatory content, not optional
- OEM part numbers build trust: reference what the part replaces
- Installation difficulty: "No cutting required. Plug-and-play installation. ~30 minutes."

### Baby / Children
- Safety certifications lead everything: ASTM, CPSC, BPA-free, phthalate-free
- Age range specifics: "Designed for 6–36 months. Choking hazard for children under 3."
- Wash instructions and material safety reassure parents

## Prohibited vs. Required Language

### Prohibited by Amazon (will suppress / remove listing)
- "Best seller" / "#1" / "top rated" without the verified badge
- "Free shipping" / "sale price" / "% off" in listing copy (only in promotions)
- "FDA approved" for supplements (FDA does not approve supplements)
- "Guaranteed" or "unconditional guarantee" in bullets
- All caps text (titles and bullets)
- Special characters (!, $, ?) in titles unless in brand name
- Competitor brand names in bullets, description, or backend keywords
- Emoji in titles

### Required in Regulated Categories (must not be removed)
- Supplement FDA disclaimer (verbatim)
- Hazard warnings: "WARNING: This product contains chemicals known to the State of California to cause cancer" (Prop 65)
- Choking hazard warnings for relevant children's products
- Country of origin for certain categories
- Age grading and safety certifications for children's products

## The Two-Pass Audit Process

### Pass 1: Pattern Kill Scan
Read the draft listing field by field (title → bullets → description → backend). For each field:
1. Highlight every word from the Pattern 7 AI vocabulary list
2. Mark every -ing clause (Pattern 3)
3. Mark every vague adjective (Pattern 4)
4. Mark every "serves as / boasts / features" construction (Pattern 8)
5. Mark any bullets in **Header: sentence** format (Pattern 15)
6. Count em dashes per bullet (Pattern 13)
7. Check for emojis (Pattern 17)

### Pass 2: The "Obviously AI" Question
After rewriting, read the full listing aloud and ask:
*"What makes this so obviously AI-generated?"*

Write down the remaining tells. Fix each one. Then output.

Common things that survive Pass 1 and get caught in Pass 2:
- All bullets start with the same length sentence
- All bullets use the same sentence structure (feature → benefit every time)
- Smooth, uniform tone with no personality variation
- Every claim is positive and nothing acknowledges any tradeoff
- Reads like it's describing a product in a vacuum rather than a product competing against 50 others

## Transformation Examples by Listing Field

### Title — Electronics Charger

❌ AI: *"Premium 65W USB-C Fast Charger — Advanced GaN Technology for Superior Charging Performance"*
- "Premium" = vague
- "Advanced" = vague
- "Superior" = unsubstantiated comparative
- "Charging Performance" = tautology (it's a charger)

✅ Human: *"Anker 65W USB-C GaN Charger, 3-Port Fast Charger, Compatible with MacBook Air/Pro, iPhone 15, Galaxy S24"*
- Brand upfront
- Wattage specific
- Technology named (GaN)
- Port count specific
- Key compatibility listed

---

### Bullet — Supplement

❌ AI: *"Experience the incredible benefits of our premium turmeric supplement, featuring our innovative BioPerine formula that enhances absorption for superior results."*
- "incredible benefits" = vague
- "premium" = vague
- "innovative" = vague
- "enhances absorption" = vague (how much?)
- "superior results" = unsubstantiated

✅ Human: *"1,500mg turmeric root extract (95% curcuminoids) per serving. 10mg BioPerine black pepper extract increases curcumin absorption by up to 2,000% vs. standard curcumin supplements (Shoba et al., 1998)."*
- Specific dose
- Standardization percentage stated
- BioPerine amount specific
- Absorption claim cited to actual research

---

### Bullet — Kitchen Knife

❌ AI: *"Crafted with precision engineering and designed for the discerning home chef, this knife showcases our commitment to culinary excellence and reflects our dedication to quality."*
- Zero information
- 4 AI vocabulary words (precision, discerning, showcases, reflects)
- Two -ing clause tail-ons (showcases, reflects)

✅ Human: *"German 1.4116 stainless steel. Blade hardness: 56–58 HRC on the Rockwell scale. Edge factory-honed to 15–17 degrees per side."*
- Steel grade specific
- Hardness rating specific
- Edge angle specific

---

### Description — Resistance Bands

❌ AI: *"Elevate your fitness journey with our premium resistance band set, designed to enhance your workout experience and help you achieve your goals. These innovative bands reflect our commitment to quality, ensuring you get the most out of every session. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced athlete, our versatile bands offer a comprehensive solution for all your fitness needs."*

Pattern count: pivotal, enhance, innovative, reflects, ensuring, versatile, comprehensive — 7 AI vocabulary words in 3 sentences. Plus: sycophantic opener, false range (beginner to athlete), vague attribution.

✅ Human: *"Set includes 5 bands: 10 lbs, 20 lbs, 30 lbs, 40 lbs, 50 lbs resistance. 100% natural latex. Each band is 41 inches / 104 cm loop diameter — fits standard pull-up bars and squat racks. Stored in the included mesh bag. If a band snaps within 12 months, send a photo and we'll replace it — no form to fill out."*

## The Soul Test

After applying all 24 patterns, run this final check:

**"Does this copy read like a knowledgeable friend who has actually used the product — or like a spec sheet assembled from product requirements?"**

Good Amazon copy knows the product has real tradeoffs and real use cases. It acknowledges who the product is for and (implicitly) who it isn't for. It's confident where the facts support confidence and honest where they don't.

**Signs copy has soul:**
- Mentions a real limitation honestly: "Runs small — size up if between sizes"
- Makes a specific claim that only someone who tested the product would know: "The zipper sticks slightly in cold weather — spray with silicone and it's fine"
- Addresses the real purchase hesitation for this category: for a $180 pan, acknowledge the price by leading with what justifies it

**Signs copy is still soulless despite being technically "clean":**
- Every sentence is positive
- No acknowledgment of tradeoffs, edge cases, or honest limitations
- All bullets are exactly the same length
- Every feature has the same structure (feature → benefit → vague positive)
- Reads like it could describe any product in this category
